Information hierarchy

The engine prioritises official operational sources. News organisations provide context and independent consensus, but do not on their own replace maritime confirmation.

Tier 2 · Reliable agencies and media

Reuters and the Associated Press receive the highest media weight, followed by the BBC, Financial Times, Bloomberg, The Guardian and other selected outlets. A news report gains strength when it explicitly attributes operational information to UKMTO, IMO, MARAD or JMIC.

Technical aggregators

GDELT and Google News RSS are used to find recent coverage. They are not treated as sources themselves: the decision depends on the identified original outlet.

What does not count as confirmation

  • Anonymous social media posts.
  • Unattributed rumours.
  • Predictions, threats or hypothetical scenarios.
  • The same article republished by many websites.
  • A headline saying “closes” without describing halted traffic or an impassable route.

A conservative automated indicator. It does not replace official navigational warnings, maritime authority instructions or professional advice.
